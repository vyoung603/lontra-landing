Before We Spend a Dollar

Product Readiness Gate

Hard truth: This plan assumes the assess-train-reassess loop delivers a genuinely compelling experience. We need evidence before turning on paid spend. Three gates:

Gate 1: First Session Experience

The AI has no patterns to surface on day one. What makes someone come back tomorrow? Our answer: the initial assessment creates a "holy shit, that's accurate" baseline moment. The AI coach uses that baseline to give specific, personalized guidance immediately. If the first session feels like ChatGPT with a warm skin, we lose 45%+ of users before the magic kicks in.

Gate 2: Beta Validation (needed ASAP)

Before launching paid: 200-500 beta users through the full assess-train-reassess loop. We need real D1/D7 retention data and at least 50 users who've hit the "reassess" moment. If retention doesn't beat category average, fix the product before spending on acquisition. No amount of marketing fixes a leaky bucket.

Gate 3: Safety Protocol

AI coach for stress and mental health = users will express crisis. Escalation path to SonderMind clinicians must be live and tested before launch. One bad screenshot goes viral and the brand is dead. Non-negotiable.

The Hard Questions

Risks, Contingencies & What Could Kill Us

Every plan has failure modes. Here are ours and what we do about them.

Risk 1: Growth Lead Hire Takes Too Long

The entire plan depends on this hire. If it takes 6-8 weeks instead of 2-3, pre-launch window is gone.

Plan B: Start with a 3-month contractor from MarketerHire or Superside. Slower, less committed, but gets ads running. Val handles creator outreach personally. Contract converts to FTE if the person is good.

Risk 2: Assessment Funnel Doesn't Convert

If the resilience quiz doesn't produce a "holy shit" moment, the entire acquisition engine stalls.

Plan B: Fall back to direct app-download ads with a free trial CTA. Less viral, more expensive, but proven. Test 3 different quiz formats before declaring it dead.

Risk 3: Meta Flags Health Ads

Facebook's health ad policies are strict. "Build your resilience" could trigger personal health claim flags. Appeals take weeks.

Mitigation: Pre-review ad copy with Meta's health ad guidelines. Avoid "you" language in claims. Have 2x creative variants ready so flagged ads don't stop spend. Budget 1 week for initial approval cycle.

Risk 4: Retention Doesn't Hit

If D7 retention is 15% instead of 30%, unit economics collapse. Paid acquisition becomes a cash furnace.

Plan B: Cut paid spend to $0. Shift entirely to organic + creator content. Use the savings to fund product improvements. Don't scale acquisition until the product retains. This is the most important rule in the plan.

Risk 5: First Session Feels Like ChatGPT

Before the AI has patterns to surface, the experience might feel generic. Users churn before the magic happens.

Mitigation: The initial assessment IS the first-session differentiator. "Here's your resilience baseline" is something ChatGPT cannot do. Then: personalized day-one coaching based on assessment results, not generic prompts. The first 5 minutes must feel different from anything else.

Pricing Strategy

Launch pricing (test both):

  • Free tier: Initial assessment + resilience score + 3 days of AI coaching. Enough to experience the loop once.
  • Premium: $9.99/mo or $79.99/yr. Full coaching, unlimited assessments, pattern recognition, progress tracking.
  • Trial: 7-day free trial for premium (standard for health apps).

Why $9.99 to start (not $14.99):

  • Lower friction for Riley (price-sensitive early career)
  • Under the psychological $10 threshold
  • Still works at conservative churn (LTV $42, CAC target $10-14)
  • Can increase to $14.99 after proving value and building social proof
  • Annual plan ($79.99) for Jane -- she'll upgrade immediately
The budget has no contingency -- and that's intentional. At $150K, there's no room for buffer. If something goes wrong (and it will), we cut scope, not quality. Kill a channel. Pause creator spend. Extend the timeline. The constraint forces prioritization. But: if we blow through $100K with no signal on retention or conversion, we stop and reassess before spending the last $50K.
